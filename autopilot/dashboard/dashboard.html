Edit Brand Profile
Business Name iBusiness NameYour official business name as you want it to appear. The AI uses this in captions and to understand context. Keep it exactly as customers know you.
Industry iIndustryYour business category. Examples: Restaurant, Retail Boutique, Hair Salon, Auto Repair, Fitness Studio. Be specific — "Coffee Shop" beats "Food Service."
Location iLocationYour city and state. The AI uses this to write posts that feel local and relevant. Example: "Willmar, MN" or "Austin, TX."
Website iWebsiteOptional but helpful for CTAs. When the AI writes "visit us online," it can reference your actual URL.
About the Business iAbout the BusinessDescribe what you do, what makes you different, and anything customers should know. The richer this is, the more specific and authentic your posts will be. Aim for 2–4 sentences.
Target Audience iTarget AudienceWho are you trying to reach? Be specific: "young professionals who want quick healthy lunches" beats "everyone." The AI will write as if speaking directly to this group.
Brand Voice iBrand VoiceHow should your posts sound? Examples: "Warm and conversational, like texting a friend." "Confident and bold — we don't apologize for being the best." "Witty with a nautical edge." This shapes every word the AI writes.
Off-Limits Topics iOff-Limits TopicsHard rules the AI will never break. Examples: "No political content." "Never mention competitor names." "Don't use the word 'cheap.'" Be specific about anything that could embarrass or misrepresent you.
Extra Context for AI iExtra ContextBackground info that makes posts feel more real and specific. Examples: "We're known for our 45-minute lunch guarantee." "Our owner has been a chef for 20 years." "We sponsor the local youth hockey team." The AI won't use this every post, but it adds authenticity.
